Desserted: The Dessert Card Game

Overview: Desserted is a strategic, fun, and engaging online card game where players combine ingredients to craft delicious desserts and score points. It's a game of wit, planning, and a dash of luck!

Key Features:
  • Multiplayer Gameplay: Compete against friends or online opponents in real-time.
  • Strategic Depth: Mix and match ingredients to create high-scoring desserts.
  • Dynamic Play: Each game is unique with a variety of ingredients and special cards.
Technologies Used:
  • Node.js, Go, PostgreSQL, gRPC, Protobuf, WebSocket
Project Structure:
  • backend/: Contains all backend Go code.
  • frontend/: Contains all frontend React code.
  • docs/: Additional project documentation.
  • tests/: Test scripts and files.

For more information, visit the GitHub repository.

About Me

Hello! I'm Alexander Merola, a passionate, thoughtful, and hard-working developer on a mission to make a meaningful impact in the tech world.

With eight rewarding years as a math teacher under my belt, I've always believed in the power of hard work, resourcefulness, and relentless pursuit of one's goals. My time in education, marked by guiding students to achieve the highest mean Algebra scores in Florida, reinforced my belief that dedication and persistence are key to success.

My time in education was not just about imparting knowledge; it was also a period of self-discovery. I encouraged my students to chase their dreams, and in doing so, I realized my growing fascination with software engineering and technology. Inspired by the rapid evolution and impact of technology in our lives, I decided to transition from education to immerse myself in the world of computer science and software engineering.

Over the past year, I've dedicated myself to mastering this new discipline, mentored by my close friend (and a senior engineer at Reddit), Andrew Garrett. I have utilized a variety of learning tools, including courses on edX such as Harvard University's CS50 and MIT's Introduction to Computer Science and Programming Using Python, UCal Berkeley's CS 61A, FastAI's "Practical Deep Learning", and a 60+ lesson "Backend Master Class" on Youtube. I have also utilized many other resources including official documentation, books such as Let's Go and The Art of Programming, LeetCode, Youtube tutorials, lessons from The Odin Project, conversations with ChatGPT, and many, many more.

My journey has equipped me with a robust skill set, ranging from Go, Python, JavaScript, React, to Swift and SwiftUI. Additionally, I've delved into industry-standard tools and technologies like AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, and Github. Perhaps more importantly, I have become extremely comfortable with working with new technologies and languages quickly, and have gained strong practical experience in many different languages and frameworks. Today, I stand confident and eager to contribute meaningfully as a software engineer .

My education was further enhanced by a six-month tenure at a pre-seed startup, where I was the sole software engineer working alongside the CEO, a childhood neighbor. This role involved transforming business ideas into a minimum viable product (MVP), developing a REST API using Go, an iOS app using Swift and SwiftUI, managing a PostgreSQL database, and implementing AWS S3 buckets with pre-signed URLs for image storage. My role also entailed implementing Authorization Code Flow with PKCE to secure the connection between backend and frontend, adding third-party sign-in capabilities with Apple and Google, creating a CI/CD pipeline with automated testing using GitHub Actions, and independently developing a 3D Building Floor Plan App using Swift, SwiftUI, UIKit, ARKit, and Appleâ€™s RoomPlan API, thereby eliminating dependency on third-party providers and associated costs. This experience was invaluable, honing my skills in practical, real-world software development and emphasizing the importance of technology and software engineering as a solution for real-world business problems and product ideas.

Steve Jobs once said that one cannot separate their work from their life, and I resonate with this philosophy deeply. I seek a position that allows me to blend my passion for technology with my desire to make a difference. I'm eager to join a team that values collaboration, innovation, and personal growth, and I look forward to using my skills to contribute to something truly amazing.
